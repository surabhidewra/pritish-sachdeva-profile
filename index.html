What happened

I co-founded Velin — a student-led technology company built within the Junior Achievement (JA) programme in Czech Republic. What started as a competition entry became a real operating business.

We competed in the JA Czech Company Competition against student ventures from across the country and secured 3rd place nationally. More significantly, Velin didn’t dissolve when the competition ended — it continues to operate today, which puts it in a rare category among student ventures.

Running Velin gave me hands-on experience in business strategy, financial modelling, product development, and team leadership that no classroom could replicate.

What happened

The BATA Shoe Innovation Challenge is an international product innovation competition hosted by BATA — one of the world’s largest footwear companies, operating in 70+ countries.

I developed a commercially viable product concept incorporating structured market research, cost-benefit analysis, manufacturing considerations, and a full investor-style pitch. The challenge wasn’t just about the idea — it was about making the idea defensible in front of a real business audience.

Finishing in the Top 10 globally against an international field was validation that the business thinking I had developed through Velin could stand up on a world stage.
