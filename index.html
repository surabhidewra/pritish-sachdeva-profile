The Story

Not just a student.
A builder who studies.

Most people my age are learning about entrepreneurship through textbooks and case studies. I was doing it in real time — co-founding Velin, navigating a live market, managing a team, and pitching to judges in a country I’d never been to before.

That experience — winning 3rd nationally in the JA Czech Company Competition and finishing Top 10 at the BATA Shoe Innovation Challenge in Prague — didn’t happen because I studied entrepreneurship. It happened because I treated every problem like a system to understand and an edge to find.

The same mindset drives my Computer Science work (grade 7), my state-level badminton, and the Economics Extended Essay I’m developing now. I don’t separate “school” from “real world.” They’re the same thing to me.

What happened

I co-founded Velin — a student-led technology company built within the Junior Achievement (JA) programme in Czech Republic. What started as a competition entry became a real operating business.

We competed in the JA Czech Company Competition against student ventures from across the country and secured 3rd place nationally. More significantly, Velin didn’t dissolve when the competition ended — it continues to operate today.

Running Velin gave me hands-on experience in business strategy, financial modelling, product development, and team leadership that no classroom could replicate.

What happened

The BATA Shoe Innovation Challenge is an international product innovation competition hosted by BATA — one of the world’s largest footwear companies, operating in 70+ countries.

I developed a commercially viable product concept incorporating structured market research, cost-benefit analysis, manufacturing considerations, and a full investor-style pitch.

Finishing in the Top 10 globally against an international field validated that the business thinking developed through Velin could stand up on a world stage.
